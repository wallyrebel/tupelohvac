Summer in Tupelo can be intense, and a functioning air conditioner is essential for comfort. If your AC runs but your house still feels warm, it’s time to troubleshoot. This guide will help you identify common issues and steps you can take before calling for professional help.  

## Why Is My AC Running But My House Still Feels Warm?  

When your air conditioner seems to be working—fans are blowing, the unit is on—but your home stays warm, several factors could be at play. Common causes include low refrigerant, dirty filters, or issues with airflow. Understanding these potential problems is the first step in fixing your air conditioner Tupelo residents rely on during hot days.  

## Troubleshooting Your AC: Practical Steps to Try  

Before scheduling a repair, try these straightforward checks:  

- **Check the Thermostat Settings**: Ensure your thermostat is set to “cool” and at a temperature lower than the current room temperature. Sometimes, settings can be accidentally changed.  
- **Replace or Clean Air Filters**: Dirty filters restrict airflow, reducing cooling efficiency. Replace filters if they’re clogged or dusty.  
- **Inspect Vents and Registers**: Make sure all vents are open and unobstructed to allow proper airflow throughout your home.  
- **Look for Ice Formation**: Check the evaporator coil for ice buildup. Ice can block heat transfer. If you see ice, turn off the AC and let it thaw.  
- **Verify External Unit Operation**: Ensure the outdoor condenser is running smoothly. Clear debris, leaves, or dirt that may block airflow around the unit.  

If these steps don’t resolve the issue, it might be time to consider professional help.

## FAQs  

**Q1: Can a dirty filter cause my AC to run but not cool my house?**  
A1: Yes, a clogged filter can restrict airflow, making it difficult for your AC to effectively cool your home.  

**Q2: How often should I check my AC’s refrigerant levels?**  
A2: Refrigerant levels should be checked during routine maintenance or if you notice poor cooling performance.  

**Q3: Is it safe to add refrigerant myself?**  
A3: No, refrigerant handling requires specialized training and equipment. It’s best to leave this to licensed professionals.
