## Why Is My AC Running but Not Cooling?

During the summer, Tupelo’s temperatures can climb into the high 80s and 90s, putting extra demand on your cooling system. When your AC runs but doesn’t cool effectively, it could be due to:

- Dirty air filters blocking airflow
- Low refrigerant levels
- Faulty thermostats
- Blocked or dirty condenser coils
- Ductwork leaks or obstructions

Addressing these issues early can prevent further damage and restore your home’s comfort.

## Troubleshooting Your AC: A Practical Checklist

Before calling for professional help, try these steps:

1. **Check the air filters:** Dirty filters reduce airflow, making your AC work harder without cooling effectively. Replace filters if they’re clogged.
2. **Inspect the thermostat:** Ensure it’s set to "cool" and at the desired temperature. Consider replacing batteries or recalibrating if necessary.
3. **Examine the outdoor unit:** Clear debris, leaves, or dirt from the condenser coils. Dirty coils hinder heat exchange.
4. **Verify refrigerant levels:** Low refrigerant can cause poor cooling. This typically requires a technician’s expertise.
5. **Inspect ductwork:** Look for visible leaks or obstructions that could be diverting cooled air away from living spaces.

If these steps don’t resolve the issue, it’s time to seek professional help to fix air conditioner Tupelo residents depend on during summer heat.

## FAQs

**Q: How often should I replace my air filters?**  
A: During summer, it's best to replace or clean filters every 1-3 months for optimal airflow and cooling efficiency.

**Q: Can low refrigerant cause my AC to run but not cool?**  
A: Yes, low refrigerant levels prevent proper heat exchange, leading to inadequate cooling despite the system running.

**Q: When should I call a professional for AC repair?**  
A: If troubleshooting doesn’t improve cooling or if you notice leaks, strange noises, or frequent cycling, contact an HVAC specialist promptly.
