As storm season approaches in North Mississippi, it’s essential for Tupelo homeowners to get their HVAC systems ready for unpredictable weather. Fall storms can bring heavy rain, wind, and power fluctuations that might impact your comfort and home safety. Proper HVAC prep can help prevent unexpected breakdowns and maintain indoor comfort when you need it most.  

## Why Storm Season Prep Matters for Tupelo HVAC Systems  

Storms can cause power surges, falling debris, and moisture intrusion—all of which can strain your HVAC system. If your system isn’t prepared, you may face costly repairs or discomfort during outages. Taking proactive steps ensures your system remains reliable and efficient through the fall storm season.  

## Practical HVAC Storm-Prep Checklist for Tupelo Residents  

Follow this simple checklist to safeguard your HVAC system:  
- **Inspect and replace filters** to ensure clean airflow and prevent strain on your system.  
- **Clear debris** around outdoor units to avoid obstruction and damage from falling branches or leaves.  
- **Secure outdoor equipment** with protective covers or barriers against wind and moisture.  
- **Check electrical connections** and consider installing surge protectors to guard against power fluctuations.  
- **Schedule a professional tune-up** to identify and fix minor issues before storms arrive.  
- **Test your backup power options** like generators to ensure your system can operate when the power is out.
