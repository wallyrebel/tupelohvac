## Why Duct and Airflow Habits Matter  

In North Mississippi, weather fluctuations can strain your HVAC system. Poor ductwork or airflow habits can lead to uneven cooling, higher energy costs, and increased wear on your system. Proper habits help distribute conditioned air evenly, prevent hot or cold spots, and extend the lifespan of your AC system.  

## Practical Habits to Improve Airflow and Efficiency  

Here are some simple, effective habits to keep your air moving efficiently:  

- **Regularly Replace or Clean Air Filters:** Dirty filters restrict airflow and force your system to work harder. Check filters monthly and replace or clean as needed.  
- **Keep Vents Clear and Open:** Ensure furniture or drapes aren’t blocking vents, which can disrupt airflow and reduce efficiency.  
- **Seal Duct Leaks:** Small leaks in ductwork can cause significant loss of cooled air. Consider having ducts inspected and sealed by professionals.  
- **Use Ceiling Fans:** Ceiling fans help circulate air within rooms, reducing the workload on your AC and maintaining more consistent temperatures.  
- **Schedule Routine Maintenance:** Regular AC service Tupelo ensures your system is clean, well-tuned, and capable of optimal airflow performance.  

## Airflow Checklist for Better Comfort

- [ ] Keep vents unobstructed

## FAQs  

**Q: How do I know if my ductwork has leaks?**  
A: Signs include uneven cooling, higher energy bills, or visible damage. An HVAC professional can perform a duct leak test.  

**Q: Can poor airflow affect my energy bills?**  
A: Yes, restricted airflow causes your system to work harder, increasing energy costs and reducing efficiency.  

**Q: Is it better to use ceiling fans or adjust the thermostat?**  
A: Using ceiling fans helps circulate air and can allow you to set your thermostat a few degrees higher without sacrificing comfort.
